How to Create a Character

Welcome to the cozy corner of character creation! Here at The Cozy Kobold, we guide you through the steps of building a character that fits your world, your story, and your table. Whether you're a daring rogue, a bookish wizard, or a lovable kobold, this guide will help you bring them to life.

Pick a Race Choose a fantasy race for your character (like human, elf, dwarf, tiefling). Each race gives you special abilities and bonuses.

Choose a Class Your class defines what your character can do. Examples: fighter (good at fighting), wizard (casts spells), rogue (sneaky), cleric (healer). Each class has unique skills and abilities.

Decide Your Ability Scores These are your character’s core stats: Strength (physical power) Dexterity (agility) Constitution (health and stamina) Intelligence (Book smarts) Wisdom (Street smarts) Charisma (charm and influence) You can roll dice, use a point-buy system, or use a standard set of numbers.

Pick a Background Backgrounds give your character a history and extra skills or tools, like being a soldier, noble, or artisan.

Select Equipment Your class and background usually give you starting weapons, armor, and tools.

Choose Spells (if applicable) If your class can cast spells (like wizard or cleric), pick your starting spells.

Fill in Details Give your character a name, personality traits, ideals, bonds, and flaws to bring them to life.

Calculate Key Stats Hit Points (how much damage you can take) Armor Class (how hard you are to hit) Attack bonuses and saving throws based on class and stats.

Rolling Your Stats

There are many ways to determine your character’s abilities! The most classic method is to roll 4d6 and drop the lowest die, but some tables prefer point buy or a standard array for balanced play. Whatever method you use, we encourage creativity—sometimes the best heroes have the quirkiest stats!
